As part of my Guided Learning module, I chose to pursue Japanese language learning with the objective of building foundational proficiency aligned with the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N5 level.

Rather than treating this as a casual interest, I structured this project around measurable learning outcomes: mastering Hiragana and Katakana, understanding fundamental grammar patterns, expanding beginner vocabulary, and improving sentence comprehension through consistent exposure.

This portfolio documents not only what I learned, but how I learned — including strategy adjustments, reflections, immersion practices, and evidence collected throughout the 17-week period.

Learning Approach & Evolution

At the start of this project, I explored traditional grammar-based study using Tae Kim’s Guide to Japanese Grammar. While this provided useful foundational explanations, I realised that reading grammar alone did not translate effectively into practical comprehension.

During the middle phase of my project, I discovered the Refold approach, which emphasises immersion and consistent exposure to the target language. From that point onward, I incorporated regular immersion into my routine by watching Japanese YouTube content (vlogs and gaming videos) and listening to Japanese music on Spotify.

Eventually, I adopted Wagotabi as my primary learning platform. Wagotabi presents Japanese in a structured, game-based format that integrates vocabulary, grammar, and sentence patterns in context. This allowed me to progress naturally through lessons while reinforcing concepts through repetition and application.

To strengthen retention, I used Anki as a supplementary spaced-repetition tool. Vocabulary and patterns encountered in Wagotabi were reinforced through review, improving recall and reading speed.

Project Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to develop foundational Japanese proficiency consistent with JLPT N5 level expectations. This includes understanding basic sentence structure, recognising polite verb forms, and reading simple beginner-level sentences with confidence.

Beyond language acquisition, this project also aimed to cultivate key personal attributes:

  • Resilience – adjusting strategies when initial methods were ineffective.
  • Inquisitiveness – researching alternative approaches and refining my learning system.
  • Effective Communication – developing the ability to interpret structured language patterns.

This portfolio serves as a comprehensive record of my learning journey, supported by documented evidence, weekly reflections, and structured progression.
